The Power of Pareto Analysis

The Pareto Principle, also known as the 80/20 rule, reveals that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes. In procurement, this typically means 80% of your spend comes from 20% of your suppliers.

Strategic Recommendations by Distribution Type

Excellent Distribution (< 20% Efficiency Ratio)

Your supplier base is highly concentrated and efficient. Focus on deepening partnerships with critical suppliers, implementing strategic supplier development programs, and exploring joint innovation opportunities. Consider long-term contracts with volume commitments for additional savings.

Good Distribution (20-40% Efficiency Ratio)

You have reasonable supplier concentration with optimization opportunities. Consider consolidating suppliers in categories with multiple vendors, strengthen relationships with top performers, and implement supplier scorecards to track performance consistently.

Fragmented Distribution (‚â• 40% Efficiency Ratio)

Your supplier base is highly fragmented, indicating significant consolidation opportunities. Prioritize supplier rationalization initiatives, evaluate category-specific consolidation potential, and implement preferred supplier programs to reduce complexity and achieve economies of scale.

High Dependency Risk (>30% Single Supplier)

Over-reliance on a single supplier creates supply chain vulnerability. Develop alternative suppliers for risk mitigation, implement dual-sourcing strategies for critical categories, and establish contingency plans to ensure business continuity.
